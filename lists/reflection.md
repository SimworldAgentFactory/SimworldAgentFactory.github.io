The reflection module is responsible for revising intermediate decisions and integrating self-improvement signals into future behavior. Starting from an initial reasoning output, it can critique the proposed action, refine the underlying rationale, or redirect the decision prior to execution. Beyond single-step adjustments, it also operates over longer trajectories by identifying recurring failure patterns, overlooked constraints, and suboptimal strategies, and incorporating these signals into subsequent decisions. By modeling reflection as a distinct component, the framework enables both step-level and trajectory-level refinement within a unified interface, allowing systematic improvement of decision quality over time through iterative critique, revision, and feedback accumulation.
